Reptile Care Guides

Learn how to care for your reptiles the right way

Snakes +

Housing: Provide an appropriately sized enclosure with secure lid and proper ventilation.

Temperature & Humidity: Maintain a thermal gradient and monitor humidity according to species needs.

Feeding: Feed pre-killed or frozen-thawed prey. Avoid handling snakes for 24-48 hours after feeding.

Handling: Be gentle, support the snakeâ€™s body, and avoid sudden movements.

Health: Regularly check for shedding issues, mites, and signs of illness.

Lizards +

Housing: Provide climbing branches, hiding spots, and a substrate suitable for the species.

Temperature & Lighting: UVB lighting is essential for vitamin D3 synthesis. Maintain a basking spot.

Feeding: Offer a balanced diet of insects, greens, and supplements where necessary.

Handling: Lizards can be stressed easily. Handle calmly and minimally at first.

Health: Watch for shedding problems, respiratory issues, and lethargy.

Turtles +

Housing: Provide an aquatic or semi-aquatic enclosure with clean water and basking area.

Temperature & Lighting: UVB lighting is necessary for shell health. Maintain water and basking temperatures.

Feeding: Offer a mix of commercial turtle food, leafy greens, and occasional protein.

Handling: Limit handling; always support their body and shell.

Health: Keep water clean, watch for shell rot and signs of infection.

Amphibians +

Housing: Provide a terrarium with humidity control, hiding spots, and a shallow water area.

Temperature & Humidity: Keep ambient temperatures stable and maintain high humidity as required.

Feeding: Feed live insects or small invertebrates appropriate for size.

Handling: Avoid handling when possible; amphibians absorb chemicals through skin.

Health: Watch for skin lesions, lethargy, or abnormal behavior.

Frogs +

Housing: Provide a secure terrarium with a shallow water area and plenty of hiding spots.

Temperature & Humidity: Maintain moderate to high humidity depending on species, and keep ambient temperature stable.

Feeding: Offer small live insects like crickets or fruit flies, dusted with calcium supplements.

Handling: Minimize handling; frogs have sensitive skin and can absorb harmful chemicals.

Health: Monitor for skin discoloration, lethargy, and abnormal swimming behavior.

Habitat Plants & Flowers +

Selection: Use non-toxic, terrarium-safe plants. Live plants improve humidity, oxygen levels, and aesthetics.

Placement: Arrange plants to provide hiding spots, climbing opportunities, and shelter for reptiles and amphibians.

Care: Water regularly, trim dead leaves, and avoid fertilizers that could harm your animals.

Flowers: Choose small, non-toxic flowering plants that are safe for enclosure inhabitants.

Enrichment: Plants and flowers enrich the habitat, reduce stress, and encourage natural behaviors.
